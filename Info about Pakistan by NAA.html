Information about Pakistan

Introduction:

Pakistan is the 33rd-largest country by area, spanning 881,913 square kilometres (340,509 square miles). It has a 1,046-kilometre (650-mile) coastline along the Arabian Sea and Gulf of Oman in the south, and is bordered by India to the east, Afghanistan to the west, Iran to the southwest, and China to the northeast.

i) Provinces:

There are four provinces of Pakistan, i.e:

  1. Punjab
  2. Sindh
  3. Balochistan
  4. Khyber Pakhtunkhwa

ii) National Flag:

The national flag of Pakistan consists of green and white colour, where green colour represents the majoritys and the white colour represents the minoritys.



iii) Capital City:

Islamabad is the capital city of Pakistan, and is administered by the Pakistani federal government as part of the Islamabad Capital Territory. It is the ninth-largest city in Pakistan. Built as a planned city in the 1960s, it replaced Rawalpindi as Pakistan's capital. The capital mosque of Pakistan Shah Faisal Masjid is also located in Islamabad, which is the 6th largest mosque in the world, with the capacity of 300,000 worshippers.



iv) Rivers:

Pakistan has one of the largest contiguous irrigation systems in the world, known as the Indus Basin Irrigation System (IBIS). The System comprises six major rivers, i.e:

  1. Indus
  2. Jhelum
  3. Chenab
  4. Ravi
  5. Sutlej
